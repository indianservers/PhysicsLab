## Completed Fluid Upgrades

| Experiment | 2D Result | 3D Result | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| `force-and-pressure` | Custom force-over-area press with adjustable piston width, pressure marks, formula, comparison badges, and gauge. | Existing specific shared 3D pressure field retained. | Adds uniform contact pressure assumption. |
| `fluid-pressure` | Custom transparent tank with depth ruler, movable probe, pressure gradient, jets, manometer, and gauge-pressure readout. | Existing specific shared 3D pressure probe retained. | Adds gauge-pressure and uniform-density assumptions. |
| `bernoulli-fluid-flow` | Existing specific 2D venturi retained. | Custom 3D venturi with wide/throat/outlet sections, pressure taps, flow particles, velocity arrows, and pressure-drop labels. | Adds steady, incompressible, viscosity-ignored, streamline-flow assumptions. |
| `buoyancy` | Existing Archimedes visual retained. | Existing buoyancy tank retained. | No rewrite; remains specific. |
| `density-float-sink` | Existing density-ratio tank retained. | Existing float/sink 3D tank retained. | No rewrite; remains specific. |

## Scientific Assumptions Added

| Experiment | Assumptions / limitations |
|---|---|
| `force-and-pressure` | Uniform contact pressure assumed; area clamped away from zero. |
| `fluid-pressure` | Gauge pressure shown; static fluid with uniform density. |
| `bernoulli-fluid-flow` | Steady flow; incompressible fluid; viscosity ignored; streamline flow. |

## Known Limitations

| Area | Limitation |
|---|---|
| Bernoulli 3D | Educational streamline visualization, not a CFD solver. |
| Force/pressure 2D | Shows uniform contact pressure; localized material deformation is not simulated. |
| Fluid-pressure 2D | Shows gauge pressure; absolute pressure is not separately visualized. |

## Recommended Phase 4 Starting Point

Start Phase 4 with thermodynamics: `heat-and-temperature`, `heat-transfer`, `gas-laws`, `thermodynamic-process`, and `calorimetry-mixing`. These are still visually repetitive in 2D and will benefit most from distinct thermometer, conduction, piston, P-V, and calorimetry apparatus.
